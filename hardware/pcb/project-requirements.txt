PROJECT TITLE
TankSync v1.0 — Solar LoRa water-tank monitor: outdoor TX + indoor RX hub for Indian homes

PROJECT REQUIREMENTS

A two-board IoT product. Hardware bench-validated on dev kits with working open-source firmware (github.com/Techposts/tanksync). I need PCBWay to convert proven pin assignments and bench wiring into manufacturable PCBs + matching enclosures + sample units.

A) TX (Tank Transmitter) — outdoor, solar-powered, mounts on overhead/sump tanks. Reads water level via ultrasonic sensor; sends readings via LoRa to indoor hub. Deep-sleeps between wakes for battery life.

B) RX (Receiver / Hub) — indoor, USB-C powered. Receives LoRa from up to 6 paired TX, drives OLED + LED ring, bridges to cloud via WiFi/MQTT, hosts local web UI.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PCB DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

TX Board:
- Read distance via ultrasonic sensor (JSN-SR04T / AJ-SR04M, waterproof)
- Sense battery + solar power via INA219 (I2C) and ADC voltage divider (variant)
- Charge 18650 Li-ion from solar via CN3791 MPPT + boost converter to 3.3V
- Communicate with RX over LoRa (RYLR998 module, UART)
- Deep-sleep between wake cycles (default 60s, configurable to 5–60 min)
- Status LED + tactile BOOT button (5s hold = AP mode for re-pairing)

RX Board:
- Receive LoRa packets from up to 6 TX units
- Drive 1.3" SH1106 OLED (I2C) for live tank levels
- Drive WS2812B LED chain (2 / 8 / 24 LEDs) for ambient indication
- Connect to home WiFi, publish telemetry to MQTT broker
- Host a local HTTP server for configuration (port 80)
- USB-C input, BOOT/RESET buttons accessible

INPUT/OUTPUT INTERFACES

TX:
- Screw terminal (2-pin) for ultrasonic sensor cable (field-installable, NOT pre-crimped at factory — sensor cable length varies by tank)
- Screw terminal or barrel jack (2-pin) for solar panel input
- 18650 battery holder
- Tactile BOOT + RESET buttons through enclosure window
- Status LED visible through enclosure window
- USB-C for development flashing

RX:
- USB-C for power + flashing
- 3-pin connector for WS2812B LED strip (5V, GND, DATA)
- 4-pin I2C header for SH1106 OLED (3.3V, GND, SDA, SCL) — display can be onboard or off-board
- BOOT + RESET tactile buttons accessible
- RYLR998 LoRa module on header (SMA antenna optional, PCB-edge whip default)

PERFORMANCE REQUIREMENTS

TX:
- MCU: ESP32-C3 SuperMini (RISC-V, 160 MHz, integrated WiFi/BT, PCB antenna)
- LoRa link: 865 MHz (India ISM band) at 22 dBm, SF7-12 configurable
- Active current: <5 mA during a wake cycle (~1.5 s of sensor read + LoRa transmit)
- Deep sleep current: <50 µA average over 60s sleep cycle
- Sensor accuracy: ±1 cm at 30 cm – 4 m range (JSN-SR04T spec)
- Battery life target: >12 months in clean Indian sun on a 1.5W panel

RX:
- MCU: ESP32 DevKit (Tensilica, 240 MHz, WiFi + BT)
- LoRa: same as TX
- I2C: 100 kHz standard mode (OLED + future I2C peripherals)
- WiFi: 2.4 GHz station mode + AP mode (for setup)
- LED refresh: 30 Hz typical, supports inrush spikes up to 1 A briefly

DIMENSIONS AND PHYSICAL SPECIFICATIONS

TX board: target ~50 × 50 mm, 1.6 mm FR-4, 4 corner mounting holes (M2.5).
RX board: target ~70 × 50 mm, 1.6 mm FR-4, 4 corner mounting holes (M2.5).
Both boards must fit inside their respective enclosures with cable routing margins.

POWER REQUIREMENTS

TX: 18650 Li-ion battery (3.0 – 4.2 V). Solar input 5 – 7 V, max 1 A. CN3791 MPPT charges battery; boost converter delivers 3.3 V regulated to ESP32-C3. Reverse-polarity Schottky diode required inline on battery + (we have lost test boards by reversing the holder). Total system current must be <100 µA during deep sleep (no quiescent draw from boost beyond MCU).

RX: USB-C 5 V / 1 A nominal input. LDO to 3.3 V for ESP32 + LoRa. WS2812B chain runs from 5 V rail directly. 1000 µF bulk cap on the 5 V rail near the LED data input — WS2812B can pull >1 A briefly when all LEDs go to white at full brightness (power-on flash, attention modes).

COMPATIBILITY REQUIREMENTS

- ESP32-C3 SuperMini and ESP32 DevKit modules are off-the-shelf parts; ours uses the standard pinouts. We need carrier boards that mount these modules (NOT redesigned MCUs — module-on-carrier is fine and is what the prototypes use).
- RYLR998 is the chosen LoRa module (UART, 865 MHz). Do not substitute without consulting us — firmware is tied to RYLR998 AT command set.
- JSN-SR04T / AJ-SR04M ultrasonic — interchangeable, both have a 4-pin header (VCC, GND, TRIG, ECHO). 5V powered, 5V echo output — voltage divider (1k + 2k to GND) on ECHO line is required because ESP32-C3 GPIOs are NOT 5V tolerant.
- INA219 at I2C 0x40 (default jumper config), 0.1 Ω shunt. Auto-detected by firmware.
- SH1106 1.3" OLED at I2C 0x3C, 128×64.

RELIABILITY

- Service life: 5+ years
- TX: -10°C to +60°C, IP65, monsoon + dust + coastal salt-haze
- RX: 0°C to +45°C, normal humidity
- Antenna keep-out: >5 mm clear copper around RYLR998 + ESP32-C3 antennas
- Decoupling: 100 nF + 10 µF within 5 mm of every IC

2. ENCLOSURE DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

TX ENCLOSURE (outdoor)
- IP65+ protection (rated for monsoon rain + rooftop dust + summer 45°C)
- Material: UV-stabilised ABS or polycarbonate (NOT plain ABS — degrades in Indian sun within 18 months)
- Cable glands: 2 (one for ultrasonic sensor cable, one for solar panel cable), M12 thread, sized for 4–6 mm cable
- Battery access: removable cover with captive screws, gasket-sealed
- Mounting tabs: 4 outside the seal, accept standard tank-lid bracket screws
- Window: small clear/translucent window for status LED visibility
- Buttons: BOOT + RESET as panel-mount tactile with rubber boots (waterproof)
- Approximate size: 80 × 80 × 40 mm internal cavity (final size depends on PCB layout + battery orientation)
- Ergonomic: must mount easily on a tank lid, no sharp edges

RX ENCLOSURE (indoor)
- IP20 (dust ingress only, no water resistance needed)
- Material: ABS or PC, smooth/matte finish acceptable
- Form factor: desktop or wall-mount, reversible hanging holes
- Window: clear cutout for OLED (or transparent panel covering whole top face)
- LED diffuser: cutout for WS2812B strip with frosted-acrylic lens or print-in-place diffuser
- USB-C port access: cutout aligned with onboard USB-C connector
- Buttons: cutouts for BOOT + RESET (recessed pin-press is fine, no rubber boots needed indoors)
- Approximate size: 100 × 60 × 30 mm
- Aesthetic: minimalist, inspired by editorial/calm UI design (the matching app uses light/serif design language)
- Colour: white or warm grey for primary; brand logo placement on top face (we will provide the logo SVG)

INTERFACE AND OPENING REQUIREMENTS

4. ENVIRONMENTAL TESTING REQUIREMENTS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

TEST OBJECTIVES

Validate that both TX and RX prototypes meet the operating-environment specs before approving for production.

ENVIRONMENTAL PARAMETERS

TX (outdoor):
- Temperature: -10°C to +60°C operating, soak test at +50°C for 4h
- Humidity: 10% to 95% RH non-condensing
- IP65 spray test per IEC 60529 (low-pressure water from any direction, 3 minutes)
- Dust ingress test: dust chamber 8h
- UV exposure: 100h accelerated (equivalent to ~1 year Indian rooftop)

RX (indoor):
- Temperature: 0°C to +45°C operating
- Humidity: 30% to 70% RH

TEST SAMPLES

2 of each board (TX + enclosure, RX + enclosure) — 4 sample units total.

PASS/FAIL CRITERIA

- Functional: pairing succeeds, LoRa link >-110 dBm at 100 m line-of-sight, OLED renders our test pattern, LED chain cycles through colours, OTA over WiFi completes
- Battery: TX active <5 mA, deep sleep <50 µA
- Mechanical: IP65 holds during spray, no water ingress on TX seal
